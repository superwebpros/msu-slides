## Discovery Interview Framework

**Your goal: Listen and learn**

<div style="font-size: 20px; line-height: 1.8; margin: 30px 0;">

1. **Business Context** - Who they serve, what makes them different

This is the framework for your discovery interview. Structured but conversational. You're learning about their business so you can build the right thing. Key sections: business context, target audience, current content landscape, goals, and access needs. Don't skip the access conversation - you need to know what URLs, files, and credentials you'll need to do your work.

### Interview Best Practices

<div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap: 30px; margin: 40px 0; font-size: 18px;">

<div style="background: #dcfce7; padding: 25px; border-radius: 10px;">
<h4 style="color: #14532d;">Do</h4>
<ul style="margin-top: 15px; line-height: 1.8;">
<li>Ask open-ended questions</li>
<li>Listen more than talk</li>
<li>Take detailed notes</li>
<li>Ask follow-up questions</li>
<li>Confirm understanding</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div style="background: #fee2e2; padding: 25px; border-radius: 10px;">
<h4 style="color: #991b1b;">Don't</h4>
<ul style="margin-top: 15px; line-height: 1.8;">
<li>Propose solutions yet</li>
<li>Dominate the conversation</li>
<li>Make assumptions</li>
<li>Skip note-taking</li>
<li>Forget to ask about access</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>

<div style="background: #fef3c7; padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0; font-size: 18px;">
<strong>Assign roles:</strong> Lead interviewer, note-taker, follow-up questions
</div>

Note:
Interview best practices. This is a listening session, not a pitch. Ask open-ended questions ("Tell me about..." not "Do you..."). Listen actively. Take good notes - you'll reference these all semester. Don't propose solutions yet - just gather context. Assign roles within your team: someone leads the questions, someone takes notes, others handle follow-ups. Everyone participates, but having clear roles prevents awkward silence or talking over each other.
